Introduction

Background

I am a college junior studying computer science. I took a UI/UX in order to learn about different aspects of CS than those covered in the classes I have taken thus far. During the first week of class, we looked at the portfolios of professional UI/UX designers. One that particularly stood out to me was that of Jesse Prague. Although she is now a product designer at Meta, many of the pieces on her website were those that she had designed while a college student and were projects specificaly geared towards fellow college students. Inspired by this, as I implemented various projects this semester, I decided to focus on designing things geared towards college students and investigating issues that my peers and I face regularly.

Projects

For my projects, I decided to focus on issues/question that other home bakers and I often have when baking. These are divided intro the following categories: ingredients, choosing recipes, managing a budget, and selling baked goods. Hover over these topics to see more specific questions

Keeping these topics terms in mind, the following are the four projects I have worked on related to investigating them further and providing some potential answers:

Personas and Storyboarding:

Because it is so conviently located relative to campus, CVS is a common stopping place for students buying snacks, daily necessities, cleaning supplies, etc. However, one issue with shopping at CVS is the self-checkout machines, which are often quite slow and finicky.

Therefore, for the personas and storyboarding assignment where we were tasked with observing users interact with an interface and developing personas and a storyboard, I chose to focus on the CVS checkout machine, as it is an interface that my peers and I interact with regularly.

Wireframing

After learning about personas and storyboarding, we moved onto prototyping, starting with LoFi wireframing. I completed this project at the beginning of the semester when there was an issue with lines in dinig halls being too long during the meal time rush, thus making it difficult for students to get food during their breaks. This inspired my to design a wireframe for a potential app that students could use to check the wait times at various dining halls in order to find a location where they would have time to get food between classes.

In our free time, many of my friends and I love to bake. However, between juggling classwork and extraciriculars, it is sometimes a struggle to find recipes that we actually have time to make. Additionally, many of us have various dietary restrictions/allergies (e.g. celiacs, lactose intolerance, etc).

Development

Overview and General Process

As mentioned above, for this project I was inspired by how my friends and I choose recipes to bake to design a WebApp that allows users to sort and filter recipes based on cooktime and dietary restrictions.

When developing this WebApp, I experimented with and learned about using separate components and state to allow users to trigger changes in the page. Specifically, users can click on the different radio buttons that determine how the data is filtered and ordered in order to change the state of the constants that determine these properties. Additionally, users can add/remove items to/from the "Favorites" list. When using state and components, I also learned about passing data through the different components via "props."
