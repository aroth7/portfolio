Introduction

Background

I am a college junior studying computer science. I took a UI/UX in order to learn about different aspects of CS than those covered in the classes I have taken thus far. Additionally, ever since I was little, I have loved to bake. Therefore, as I learned about the different aspects of UI/UX work, I decided to focus my projects on topics related to the different aspects of baking in college: buying ingredients, finding recipes, managing a budget and splitting costs with friends, and potentially selling baked goods to others.

Projects

For my projects, I decided to focus on issues/question that other home bakers and I often have when baking. These are divided intro the following categories: ingredients, choosing recipes, managing a budget, and selling baked goods. Hover over these topics to see more specific questions

Keeping these topics terms in mind, the following are the four projects I have worked on related to investigating them further and providing some potential answers:

Purchasing Ingredients:

As a college student, I often buy ingredients at CVS. It is reletively cheap, thus helping me stay within budget. More importantly it is very conviently located and doesn't require prior planning to go to, allowing me and my friends to spontaneously decide to bake something when we want to without having to worry about not having a lot of time to buy ingredients.

However, one issue with CVS is the checkout machine, which is often quite slow and finicky. Therefore, this first project uses personas and storyboarding to investigate experiences and common issues that various users have while interacting with said machine.

Choosing Recipes

Another common issue that faced by me and my other friends who like to bake is choosing recipes that fit the amount of time we have, as well as finding recipes that are work for people with dietary restrictions, such as those who are gluten free or lcatose intolerant. Therefore, when we learned about developing webapps,I decided to design one that allows users to filter and sort recipes based on cooktime and dietary restrictions, thus helping them find recipes that fit whatever constraints they might have.

Managing a Budget and Splitting Costs

Another concern for college bakers (and really college students in general) is managing budgets and splitting the cost of things with friends. For example, when my friends and I bake together, it is often hard to coordinate who is paying for which ingredients. Therefore, for iterative design, my group decided to work on a prototype of an app for a startup called Orca, which is focused on providing one location where gen Z users can manage their budgets, pay friends, split checks, etc.

Iterative Design

As mentioned above, for this project, my group chose to design a mockup of an App for the startup Orca, which is focused on providing one location where gen Z users can manage their budgets, pay friends, split checks, etc.

When designing the app, we first discussed a variety a different sketches, which culminated in the making of a LoFi wireframe. We then moved onto making our HiFi prototype in Figma. This prototype went through a few iterations where we incorpoated peer feedback. We also submitted the HiFi prototype to a user testing website to investigate how actual potential users would interact with it to complete a series of tasks we had designed.

Since this startup's app is aimed at Gen Z users, while working on potential designs, I thought about how my friends and I might use the app to manage our own budgets and split bills/checks together.
